Privacy Policy

Every time you visit Custom Tie Designer - meaning us, our site, or whatever name fits - we take steps to keep your personal details safe. Our way of gathering data comes through your visits here, what you click on, and how you move around. Protection isn't just a note at the bottom; it shapes how everything runs behind the scenes. Knowing what happens to your info matters because it stays part of every choice we make. Take a moment now, go ahead, look this over - it explains things clearly without hiding behind fine print.

Information we collect

No one gathers your private details when using the tie maker. Right inside your web app, every pick - like hues, prints, or shirt neck shapes - and anything kept, say a file saved as JSON or pictures exported as PNGs, stays put on your machine unless sent off by you. Nothing about what you build ever reaches our systems, gets held there, or moves through them.

Sometimes outside tools show up on our site. Google AdSense runs ads, while Google Analytics tracks visitor behavior. Because of this, certain details like overall visit numbers or what kind of device someone uses might get gathered. Each tool follows its own rules about privacy when doing so. Your private info stays with us - it does not go to others for sale. If you want specifics on how they manage that data, look at Google's Privacy Policy along with each service's individual policy.

Cookies and storage

Most times, websites keep small bits of info on your device. That happens using tools like cookies or storage spots built into browsers. Their main job? Making things run smoother for you. Say you pick a theme or language - those choices might stick around. It is done so pages work as they should. Never are private details saved that way unless you say it is okay. If something feels off, adjustments live inside your browser. Change what gets kept. Remove it entirely. Entirely up to you.

Data security

Your designs face their biggest danger right where you work - on your device. Since nothing gets saved on our servers, protection falls to how safe your system stays. Keeping your browser updated helps guard what matters. Important files, like JSON or PNG versions, should live in more than one place. Think ahead: losing a file hurts less when copies exist elsewhere.

Wherever you're based, rules around your personal information might apply - like seeing it, fixing errors, or asking for removal.
